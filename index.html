Overview

The home robot has long been promised but not yet realized. Unlike manipulation-focused robots in a table-top setting, home robots need to operate in larger spaces, necessitating advancements in mobile manipulation. They also face the challenge of reasoning and planning over longer horizons. Other interesting aspects to consider are the type of scene representations to be used for homes and how we expect robots to interact with human inhabitants. Robot learning approaches hold the promise of providing excellent generalization and adaptation abilities. The objective of this workshop is to discuss what learning approaches we need to focus on that would be most effective for robot operation in diverse home environments. The intended audience for this workshop is researchers and practitioners working towards robot application in unstructured environments such as homes, with a special focus on learning-based approaches. The target communities and presenters include researchers in the fields of: Mobile Manipulation, Robot Learning, Robot Perception, Long-Horizon Planning and (Scene) Representation Learning.

  • How do we generalize our robot behaviors to diverse homes?
  • What scene representations should we be using for home environments?
  • Can end-users use learning to pernsonalize/adapt robot behavior to suit their needs?
  • How do we learn Mobile Manipulation skills? What is the best way to combine mobility, manipulation and embodied perception?
  • What datasets & benchmarks do we need to make progress in home robotics?
