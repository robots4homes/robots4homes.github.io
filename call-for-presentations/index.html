Call for Contributions

We are excited to invite PhD students and postdoctoral researchers to present their published works or ongoing research as spotlight presentations and posters at our upcoming workshop on Robot Learning for Homes. The spotlight presentations and posters at this workshop aim to provide a platform for young researchers to showcase their cutting-edge work, share their insights, and foster vibrant discussions across the Robotics community.

We encourage submissions from researchers at various stages of their academic journey, from early-stage PhD students to recent postdocs. We will consider diversity and relevance of the topics. This is an excellent opportunity to receive valuable feedback, network with experts and peers in the field, and gain visibility for your research.

As we review the submissions, we'll be searching for diverse ideas that stand out. Our aim is to create a workshop program that's more than just a gathering â€“ it's like a dynamic quilt of ideas, where each piece contributes to exciting research discussions.

Presentation Formats

Note: We prefer presentations to be in-person.

  • Spotlight Presentations. Selected researchers will have the chance to deliver spotlight talks, providing concise and impactful overviews of their work. Spotlight presentations will be part of focused sessions, highlighting key findings and contributions to the field. Presenters will have the undivided attention of the audience, creating a dynamic and engaging atmosphere. Submissions selected as spotlight presentations will automatically be considered for poster presentation. Hence, an additional poster presentation is also required.
  • Poster Presentations. Presenters will have the opportunity to showcase their research in a visual and interactive format. Posters will be displayed in a dedicated poster hall, providing an engaging environment for discussions and knowledge exchange. Attendees will have ample time to explore the posters and interact with presenters during the designated poster session.

Submission Guidelines

  • Submissions should be in the form of 2-page research statements OR 4-page extended abstracts (excluding references | PDF | Max file size - 10 MB | RSS24 Latex Template) that provides an overview of the research topic, key objectives, methodologies, and preliminary or expected results.
  • Submissions can include a list of published papers of the applicant relevant to the workshop.
  • Submissions should indicate if the applicant is presenting their work at RSS24 as part of the main conference.
  • Please specify your preferred presentation format (poster or spotlight) in the submission.
  • Please include the publication venue of your work as a part of submission.
  • Please note that all presentations should be relevant to the workshop theme and align with the conference's code of conduct.
  • Use this form for your submission.

Some points to include in the submission

  • Motivation: Motivation behind the research question(s) addressed in the applicant's studies.
  • Objectives: Clarity in defining and scoping the problem with assumptions.
  • Related work: A brief review of related work on the aforementioned research question(s).
  • Approach or Method(s): A description of approaches contributed by the applicant, their novelty, and potential advantages over existing ones.
  • Significance of the results: What did the experiments result in? How significant are these results?
  • Open Challenges and Future Work: What obstacles are yet to be overcomed? Or what inquiries remain to be addressed with greater specificity?
